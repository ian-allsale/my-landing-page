Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about space travel

All passengers undergo comprehensive pre-flight training tailored to their mission. This includes zero-gravity simulations, emergency procedures, spacecraft systems familiarization, and physical conditioning. Training duration ranges from 3 days for orbital flights to 6 months for Mars expeditions.

Passengers must pass a medical examination that includes cardiovascular health checks, blood pressure screening, and general fitness assessment. While you don't need to be an athlete, you should be in good overall health and free from conditions that could be aggravated by g-forces or zero gravity.

Yes, we offer flexible booking options. Cancellations made 90+ days before departure receive a 75% refund. You can reschedule once without penalty up to 60 days before your flight. Please review our complete Terms & Conditions for full details.

Your ticket includes all pre-flight training, space-rated flight suit, meals during the mission, comprehensive insurance coverage, medical support, ground transportation to launch facility, and post-flight celebration. Personal items and special requests may incur additional fees.

Safety is our absolute priority. Our spacecraft undergo rigorous testing and meet all international space safety standards. We maintain a 99.8% mission success rate, and all vehicles feature redundant safety systems, emergency abort capabilities, and real-time ground monitoring throughout every flight.
